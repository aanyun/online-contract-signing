CHANGE ORDER PROCES

 

A change order is a binding agreement that modifies the original contract. Change orders may be issued for the following reasons:

  • To modify the scope of the original agreed upon work
  • To revise the contract deadline
  • To revise the terms and conditions of the original contract

 

Standard Procedure

 

If a project change is requested by the Manufacturer and/or Chain, the project manager will determine the course of action depending on whether or not the change requested is within the original scope of the project.

If the change requested is inside of the initial scope of the project, the project manager will instruct the production team to make the change as requested.

If the change requested is outside of the initial scope of the project, the project manager will email the Manufacturer and/or Chain with the change, the process that Ignitor Labs will take to make the change, an estimated time of completion, as well as any incurred charges created by the change at a rate of {{info.client.rate | currency}} per hour. This also applies to a Change Order triggered by any accumulated research on the part of Ignitor Labs due to required source materials being inadequate or incomplete. The Manufacturer and/or Chain will be required to give written approval of the change order. Once approval is received, the project manager will instruct the production team to make the change as requested. Once the changes have been made, the Manufacturer and/or Chain will be billed for the changes as discussed, dependent on who requested the change.

 

Example

The Manufacturer and/or Chain e-mails the project manager and requests a change in script after approval has been given. The project manager will provide a written quote, a change order process, and an estimated time of completion to the Manufacturer and/or Chain for the change. The Manufacturer and/or Chain will either approve or decline the quote for the change via e-mail. If the Manufacturer and/or Chain declines, no changes will be made. If the Manufacturer and/or Chain approves the quote, the project manager will instruct the production team to make the change. Once the change is completed and approved by the Manufacturer and/or Chain, the Manufacturer and/or Chain will be billed for the additional charges incurred.
